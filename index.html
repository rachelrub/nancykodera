Bio

Nancy Kodera has worked with children from 1999 when she started as a parent volunteer at the Apple Valley Montessori School in Sudbury, Massachusetts where her two children went to school until their elementary years. The following year, she worked as assistant teacher. After working as assistant teacher for many years, her passion for Montessori continued to grow even after her children had graduated from college. She took her A to I (Assistant to Infancy) training at the Montessori School of San Diego (MISD) with Dr. Silvia Dubovoy in 2012. When Apple Valley closed in 2013, she was immediately hired to work as a lead teacher at the eBridge Montessori school. She finished her Master of Education degree at Loyola University in Maryland in 2014. In 2019 she got admitted to the AMI (Association Montessori International) Training of Trainers Program.

Passionate about children, Nancy Kodera gave a presentation on “Toileting Awareness and Independence” during the Montessori Schools of Massachusetts conference held at Dean College in Franklin, MA, January 2020. She believes that children who are functionally independent at an early age do better in the future. It takes prepared environment (human and physical) to make this possible. She looks forward to sharing with other adults strategies and ways to support the child’s independence in toileting and in other areas of their daily life.
